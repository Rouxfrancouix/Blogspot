Dooars Travel Public Relations (PR) Strategy


Flagship Wildlife Destinations

Gorumara National Park & Jaldapara National Park: Showcase Rhinos (one-horned, especially), elephants, bison, and rare birds like Bengal florican. Strong visuals for wildlife travel PR.

Chapramari & Buxa Tiger Reserve: Emphasize their role as prime elephant corridors and offbeat trekking zones for adventure storytelling.


Offbeat Nature & Eco‑Tourism Hubs

Murti, Rocky Island: Highlight riverside camping, starry nights, and serene escapes.

Loleygaon, Samsing, Suntalekhola, Neora Valley: Underscore peaceful trails, tea plantations, birding spots, and tranquil forest stays.


Tea & Heritage Experiences

Nagrakata, Meteli, Madarihat, Mungpoo: Position these as culturally rich hubs—gazette colonial bungalows, Cinchona tea plantations, artisanal practices.


Engaging Itineraries & Accessibility

Frame 4‑5 day tour formats: NJP/Bagrao → wildlife parks → forest trails → offbeat villages/custom safaris.

Highlight excellent connectivity: rail/road access via Siliguri/NJP, scenic drives with hairpin turns, shared transport options.


Budget‑Friendly & Niche‑Targeted

Emphasize low-cost travel: affordable accommodation, economical safari, boating options.

Target segments: solo travellers, families, birdwatchers, eco-tourists, heritage seekers, Buddhist, cultural followers.


Storytelling Tips for PR Success

Rich storytelling: Use immersive narratives highlighting sound, smell, emotion of forest, landscapes, community.

Media & influencer synergy: Host fam trips, pitch unique angles like “gateway to Bhutan,” tea-trail tales, or grassroots eco-tourism.

Tactics: Develop punchy headlines, localized narratives, and asset-rich kits (photos, videos, infographics); build press/media relations with experiential hooks.


Sample PR Narrative Outline

1. Hook: “Dooars: where the jungle’s whisper meets colonial charm and a rhino’s trumpet.”

2. Overview: Blend flagship national parks with scenic tea villages and hidden forest stays.

3. Highlights: Rhino safaris in Gorumara & Jaldapara. Campfires at Murti’s riverside under cosmic skies. Heritage walk through colonial gardens of Mungpoo’s Cinchona estate.

4. Key messages: Accessible routes, budget‑friendly stays.
Conservation ethos: wildlife, local tribes, sustainable tourism.
Niche appeal: birders, trekkers, culture seekers.

5. Call to Action: Encourage media fam trips, influencer visits, or blog-series takeovers.


Strategic Tools

Social Media Campaign

"Discover Dooars" – photo contests, reels, stories featuring hidden gems.

Promote Dooars as a prime eco-tourism and cultural travel destination in North Bengal, India.


Target Audiences

  • Urban Indian travelers (Kolkata, Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru)
  • International eco-tourists and birdwatchers
  • Travel influencers and bloggers
  • Domestic tour operators

Key Messages

  • “Dooars” – The gateway to the forests of Bhutan and North Bengal
  • Explore pristine forests, tea gardens, and diverse wildlife (Jaldapara, Gorumara)
  • Experience authentic tribal culture, cuisine, and homestays
  • Ideal for nature lovers, wildlife photographers, and peace seekers

PR Tactics

  • Press Releases: Feature new eco-tourism packages, events (like River Rafting Festival in Teesta), conservation efforts
  • Media FAM Trips: Invite journalists and travel bloggers for immersive experiences
  • Influencer Collaborations: Partner with travel influencers for Instagram/YouTube vlogs
  • Event Tie-ins: Participate in travel fairs, expos (like TTF India)

PR Collaterals

  • Press kit (with high-res images, destination map, factsheet)
  • Video promo and testimonials from travelers
  • Brochures in multiple languages

Measurement

  • Media coverage (print, online, broadcast)
  • Social media reach and engagement
  • Increase in tourist footfall (data from local tourism boards)
