The Hungry Notebook

Exploring flavours, one bite at a time

23/B, Sector 5, Salt Lake, Kolkata – 700004

Restaurant Review

There’s a quiet charm in discovering a local gem like The Hungry Notebook — a modest Indian street food joint that delivers big on flavour without burning a hole in your wallet.

Tucked in a corner of Maple Avenue, its vibe is unpretentious: metal tables, Bollywood tunes, and a short-but-savvy menu of Indian street classics — from kathi rolls to kulfi. The prices? Nothing over ₹250. A rare delight.

What I Tried:

  • Butter Chicken Kathi Roll (₹180): Rich, smoky, and packed with tender chicken in a flaky paratha ★★★★☆
  • Pani Puri (₹90): Crisp, spicy, and bold, served traditionally. ★★★★☆
  • Masala Chai (₹40): Aromatic perfection in a clay cup. ★★★★★
  • Mango Kulfi (₹60): Sweet, creamy, and nostalgia on a stick. ★★★★☆

Total: ₹370 — for a full-course street food experience!

Service was friendly, portions satisfying, and every dish tasted like a homemade recipe. Sure, the ambiance is simple, but that’s part of the charm.

Final Scores:

  • Food: 4.5/5
  • Portion: 4/5
  • Price: 5/5
  • Ambiance: 3.5/5
  • Overall: ★★★★½

Would I return?
Definitely. For anyone craving soulful Indian snacks on a budget, The Hungry Notebook is a flavorful find worth bookmarking.

🍽️ Restaurant Food Public Relations Strategy

A successful restaurant PR strategy blends storytelling, social engagement, and local connections to drive visibility and foot traffic. Here’s a concise plan to position your restaurant as a must-visit destination.

🎯 Goals

  • Boost local and online brand awareness
  • Drive dine-in and online reservations
  • Build influencer and media relations
  • Foster loyalty and increase customer engagement

🧭 Brand Positioning

Establish your restaurant as a hub of authentic, high-quality cuisine and warm hospitality — a community favourite that delivers more than just meals.

🎯 Target Audience

  • Primary: Local diners, working professionals, and families
  • Secondary: Tourists, bloggers, and lifestyle influencers
  • Tertiary: Corporate clients, event planners, and media

🛠️ Key PR Tactics

  • Host influencer dinners and tasting events
  • Send press kits to local food journalists monthly
  • Collaborate with food delivery platforms
  • Pitch stories around your chef, sustainability, or signature dishes
  • Launch seasonal food campaigns and themed events

✍ Content Creation

  • Photos: Dish shots, ambiance, diners
  • Videos: Prep behind-the-scenes, chef talks, time-lapses
  • Copy: Captions, testimonials, blogs
  • UGC: Tagging, sharing, and reviews

📱 Social Media Strategy

  • Platforms: Instagram, Facebook, Google, YouTube Shorts
  • Weekly Routine: Engage with food posts, respond to DMs/comments, run polls and contests

📆 Weekly Content Plan

Day Content Type
Monday📸 Dish Spotlight
Tuesday🎥 Cooking Reel
Wednesday🧑🍳 Chef Story
Thursday📸 Throwback or Memory Post
Friday🎉 Special/Offer Reel
Saturday📍 Geo-Tagged Post
Sunday🤝 UGC/Influencer Repost

📍 Geo & Influencer Outreach

  • Use local hashtags
  • Partner with 5–10 micro-influencers monthly
  • Offer complimentary meals for content
  • Encourage reviews on Google and TripAdvisor

📊 Success Metrics

  • Media coverage and mentions
  • Engagement rate and UGC volume
  • Reservation count and influencer ROI
  • Customer repeat visits
